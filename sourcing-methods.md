# Sourcing Methods

## 1. DSIR Recognition Database

### Why it works
DSIR-recognized companies usually have:
- strong R&D capability,
- differentiated products,
- and technical leadership.

Useful for:
- biotech,
- diagnostics,
- specialty chemicals,
- instrumentation.

### Limitation
Some strong companies never apply for DSIR recognition.

---

## 2. CDSCO Regulatory Filings

Useful for finding:
- IVD manufacturers,
- diagnostic kit companies,
- regulated device manufacturers.

Regulatory approvals strongly indicate real manufacturing capability.

### Limitation
Data quality can be inconsistent.

---

## 3. Industry Trade Shows

Examples:
- BioAsia
- India Lab Expo
- CPHI India
- analytica Anacon India

### Why it works
Exhibitor lists naturally concentrate:
- active companies,
- exporters,
- scaling manufacturers.

### Limitation
Requires post-event qualification.

---

## 4. Export Databases

Sources:
- Volza
- Zauba
- ImportGenius

### Why it works
Export activity strongly correlates with:
- differentiated manufacturing,
- process maturity,
- growth.

### Limitation
Product descriptions can be vague.

---

## 5. LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Useful for:
- founder research,
- hiring signals,
- manufacturing keywords,
- growth activity.

### Limitation
Some SMEs have limited LinkedIn presence.

---

## 6. Patent Databases

Sources:
- Google Patents
- InPASS

### Why it works
Patents strongly indicate:
- technical differentiation,
- process innovation.

### Limitation
Not all SMEs patent their work.

---

## 7. Industry Associations

Examples:
- ABLE
- IDMA
- CII
- FICCI

### Why it works
Associations cluster:
- active,
- growth-oriented,
- technically sophisticated manufacturers.

### Limitation
Needs filtering to remove large corporates.
