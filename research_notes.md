# Research Notes

## Observations

### 1. Large Number of False Positives
Many companies initially appearing as biotech manufacturers were actually:
- CROs,
- testing labs,
- importers,
- or distributors.

---

### 2. Manufacturing Verification Was Critical
The strongest signal was explicit evidence of:
- production facilities,
- clean rooms,
- GMP plants,
- or fermentation infrastructure.

---

### 3. Founder Quality Correlated Strongly With Differentiation
Scientist-led companies were more likely to:
- have proprietary products,
- export globally,
- invest in R&D,
- and show technical depth.

---

### 4. Trade Show Exhibitor Lists Were High-Signal
Industry expos provided concentrated datasets of:
- active manufacturers,
- exporters,
- and scaling companies.

---

### 5. Common Disqualifiers
Most rejected companies failed due to:
- being service firms,
- lack of manufacturing,
- generic pharma exposure,
- PE ownership,
- or no visible activity.
